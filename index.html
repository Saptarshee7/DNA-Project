Understanding DNA

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the molecule that carries genetic instructions used in the growth, development, functioning, and reproduction of all known living organisms.

Structure of DNA

DNA consists of two strands forming a double helix. These strands are made of nucleotides composed of a phosphate group, a sugar molecule, and a nitrogen base.

Function of DNA

DNA contains instructions for building proteins, essential for life. It also plays a key role in inheritance and evolution.
